02 — Inside my thesis · Interactive

Guiding Stem Cells Toward a Cardiac Fate

Over 6 days I differentiate mouse embryonic stem cells (mESCs) via the hanging-drop method, tracking the lineage markers that appear at each stage by immunofluorescence — then test whether the phthalate metabolite MEHP disrupts commitment. Step through the real protocol, or hit play, and switch the condition to see the comparison my work investigates.

🧾 Thesis conclusion

Using this in vitro model, MEHP disrupts early mesoderm and cardiac mesoderm differentiation. After exposure at an environmentally relevant dose, significantly fewer cells were positive for T/BRACHYURY (pan-mesoderm), MESP1 (cardiac mesoderm), and the T⁺MESP1⁺ transition population — pointing to impaired mesoderm induction and weaker progression toward cardiac lineage commitment. The stronger effect at the lower, environmentally relevant dose suggests sensitivity to low concentrations.

Some of this change was shared with the DMSO vehicle control, so the solvent contributes in part; but MEHP reduced marker expression further than vehicle alone, indicating an additional effect of its own. MEHP also showed a dose-dependent trend toward increased apoptosis (cPARP), though this was not statistically significant and needs larger studies to confirm.
