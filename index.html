02 — Inside my thesis · Scroll to develop

Guiding stem cells toward a cardiac fate.

Over six days I differentiate mouse embryonic stem cells (mESCs) by the hanging-drop method, tracking the lineage markers that appear at each stage by immunofluorescence — then test whether the phthalate metabolite MEHP disrupts commitment. Every cell you see forming the heart follows this real protocol. Scroll to build it, and switch the condition to see the comparison my work investigates.

Thesis conclusion

Using this in vitro model, MEHP disrupts early mesoderm and cardiac mesoderm differentiation. After exposure at an environmentally relevant dose, significantly fewer cells were positive for T/BRACHYURY (pan-mesoderm), MESP1 (cardiac mesoderm), and the T⁺MESP1⁺ transition population — pointing to impaired mesoderm induction and weaker progression toward cardiac lineage commitment. The stronger effect at the lower dose suggests sensitivity to low concentrations.

Some of this change was shared with the DMSO vehicle control, so the solvent contributes in part; but MEHP reduced marker expression further than vehicle alone, indicating an added effect of its own. MEHP also showed a dose-dependent trend toward increased apoptosis (cPARP), though this was not statistically significant and needs larger studies to confirm.

M.Tech Thesis2025 — 2026

Phthalate exposure & congenital heart disease

SRM Institute of Science and Technology

Investigating how DEHP — an endocrine-disrupting chemical — disrupts cardiac development, using an in vitro mouse embryonic stem cell model.

Key result: MEHP significantly reduced T/BRACHYURY, MESP1, and T⁺MESP1⁺ cells — impaired early mesoderm and cardiac-mesoderm commitment.

  • Studying the impact of MEHP (Mono-(2-ethylhexyl) phthalate) on cardiac differentiation of mouse embryonic stem cells (mESCs).
  • Cultured mESCs and performed cardiac differentiation assays to assess EDC toxicity.
  • Generated embryoid bodies using the hanging drop method to model early embryonic development and cardiac lineage specification.
  • Evaluated developmental progression through cardiac progenitor markers (T and MESP1) and immunofluorescence.
  • Investigated effects of environmental toxicants on cell-fate specification and cardiac developmental pathways.

Eco-friendly quantum dots for bioimaging

NMAM Institute of Technology

Synthesized organic quantum dots from natural materials — lignin, chitosan, honey, and nanocellulose — for bioimaging and antimicrobial applications.
